Board Games

A D20 (20 sided dice) is used in many board games and Tabletop Roleplaying Games (TTRPGs), such as Dungeons & Dragons.

History of Board Games:

There are many types of board games, which can involve elements of luck, strategy and diplomacy. Some rely entirely on one element (such as chess), while others use a combination of the three.
Themes can include horror, nature, trains, farming, religion and many others.
Games can be co-operative, competitive, solo, or semi co-operative - such as games with a traitor aspect

The oldest board games in the world are Chess (600AD, India), Nine men's Morris (1400BCE, unknown), Go (2000 BCE, Ancient China), The Royal Game of Ur (2400 BCE, Ancient Mesopotamia), Mehen (2700 BCE, Ancient Egypt), Backgammon (3000BCE, Ancient Persia), Checkers (3000BCE, Ancient Mesopotamia), and Senet (3000BCE, Ancient Egypt).
There are now hundreds of thousands of board games played worldwide, from thousands of different publishers.

Evolution of Board Games

While board games are traditionally played around a table, there are now many online versions of board games on sites like Boardgamearena, Tabletopia on steam, and on the app store.
There are also many board game shops and board game cafes where you can buy and play different board games.
